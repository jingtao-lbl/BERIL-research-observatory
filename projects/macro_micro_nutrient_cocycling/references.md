# Literature References: Macro-Micro Nutrient Gene Co-cycling in Bacterial Pangenomes

**Review depth**: Standard review
**Papers found**: 55+ total | **Full text read**: 4 | **Abstract only**: 12 | **Sources searched**: PubMed (8 queries), bioRxiv, Google Scholar, citation snowball (10 seeds)
**Date**: 2026-05-08

---

## Summary

The literature strongly supports the biological plausibility of macro-nutrient and trace-metal gene co-occurrence in bacterial genomes. The McRose & Newman (2021) discovery that phenazines — regulated by phosphorus limitation — dissolve iron oxyhydroxides to release adsorbed phosphate provides direct mechanistic evidence for P-metal coupling at the metabolite level. Subsequent work by McRose et al. (2023) and LeTourneau et al. (2019) extends this to the rhizosphere, showing that phenazine chemistry is tuned to anoxic/microaerobic conditions and that PCA-producing bacteria measurably enhance Fe mineral reactivity and crop nutrition. At the genomic level, several pangenome studies find that nutrient acquisition genes (especially phosphate transport and metal handling) are among the most variable components of accessory genomes and are subject to strong selection driven by environmental nutrient limitation (Moulana et al. 2020; Grote et al. 2012). However, no prior study has systematically quantified co-occurrence of macro-nutrient and trace-metal gene families across thousands of species pangenomes, making the present BERDL analysis a novel contribution.

## Key Findings by Theme

### Theme 1: Phenazine–Iron–Phosphorus Coupling

This theme provides the primary mechanistic foundation for the project's hypothesis.

- **McRose & Newman (2021)** — Showed that phenazine production by pseudomonads is upregulated under P limitation, that phenazines dissolve iron oxides to release adsorbed phosphate, and that this increases P-limited microbial growth. This is the central paper motivating the project's hypothesis. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abd1515) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **McRose, Li & Newman (2023)** — Extended the 2021 work to compare coumarins (plant RAMs) and phenazines (bacterial RAMs). PCA functions best under anoxic/microaerobic, near-neutral pH conditions for Fe(III) reduction, while coumarins are more effective under oxic, acidic conditions. Demonstrates complementary chemical ecological niches. Key finding: the "paradox of secondary metabolite redundancy" is resolved by considering spatiotemporal variation in rhizosphere redox conditions. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2217951120) `[FULL TEXT]`

- **Alcalde et al. (2025)** — Demonstrated that phosphorus stress and spatial confinement lower the quorum-sensing threshold for phenazine production, providing a regulatory mechanism linking P availability to phenazine biosynthesis at the cell-density level. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.11.11.687776) `[ABSTRACT ONLY — PMC text unavailable]`

- **LeTourneau et al. (2019)** — PCA-producing bacteria enhance iron mineral-associated phosphorus reactivity in wheat rhizospheres. The first field-validated demonstration that phenazine producers alter mineral-P cycling in an agricultural context. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1111/1462-2920.14341) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **LeTourneau et al. (2018)** — PCA promotes biofilm development and slows turnover of rhizobacterial biomass on wheat root surfaces under dryland conditions. PCA biosynthesis genes are upregulated under low-moisture conditions. Establishes the rhizosphere biofilm context for phenazine-mediated nutrient cycling. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1111/1462-2920.14244) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **Mazurier et al. (2009)** — Phenazine-producing Pseudomonas populations contribute to natural soil suppressiveness against Fusarium. Establishes phenazine production as a competitively significant trait in soil microbial communities, consistent with the project's finding that phenazine operon carriage predicts metal-gene co-occurrence. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.soilbio.2009.01.006) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

### Theme 2: Redox-Active Metabolites and Iron Mineral Dissolution

- **Goswami et al. (2024)** — Developed a phospho-ferrozine assay to screen bacteria for redox-active metabolite (RAM) production under phosphorus stress. Found that RAM production under P limitation is more widespread than previously appreciated, extending beyond pseudomonads to diverse soil bacteria. Directly relevant to the project's finding of broad P×Metal gene co-occurrence across bacterial phyla. PMID: 39688434 [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1128/aem.02194-24) `[FULL TEXT]`

### Theme 3: Pangenome Analysis and Nutrient Gene Variation

These studies provide methodological and conceptual precedent for the project's pangenome-scale approach.

- **Moulana et al. (2020)** — Showed that selection is a significant driver of gene gain and loss in the Sulfurovum pangenome at hydrothermal vents. Phosphate regulation genes (including ABC-type phosphate transporters) were enriched in Mid-Cayman Rise genomes from the low-phosphate Atlantic Ocean. Rare genes undergo stronger positive selection than conserved genes. Directly supports the project's finding that phosphate acquisition genes (especially phoD) show accessory-genome behavior consistent with HGT. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1128/mSystems.00673-19) `[FULL TEXT]`

- **Grote et al. (2012)** — SAR11 has the most conserved core genome of any known free-living bacteria (48-56% core). Variation among genomes included phosphorus metabolism, glycolysis, and C1 metabolism, suggesting adaptive specialization in nutrient resource utilization drives ecotype divergence. Demonstrates that P metabolism genes are among the most variable in streamlined marine genomes. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1128/mBio.00252-12) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **Tiruveedula & Wangikar (2017)** — Proposed "Gene Conservation Index" (GCI) as a predictor of gene essentiality in cyanobacteria. Identified clusters of co-evolving genes across 23,643 non-redundant cyanobacterial genes. Methodologically relevant: the co-evolution analysis parallels the project's co-occurrence approach, though applied within a single phylum rather than across GTDB. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0178565) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **Xie et al. (2025)** — Global metagenomic analysis of Ca. Accumulibacter (phosphorus-removing bacteria) across 828 metagenomes. Pangenome analysis revealed clade-specific gene gain/loss in nitrogen metabolism, genome streamlining, and metabolic complementarity. Demonstrates how pangenome-scale analysis can reveal nutrient metabolism evolution in environmentally important organisms. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/ismejo/wraf278) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **Xie et al. (2024)** — Used pangenome + protein structure prediction to annotate "genomic dark matter" in Ca. Accumulibacter. Found a phosphate-specific transport accessory protein (Pap) and identified complete denitrification pathways in additional lineage members. Directly relevant to the challenge of gene family definition and annotation sensitivity. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.4c09948) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **Jung et al. (2023)** — Chryseobacterium comparative genomics showing phosphorus- and zinc-cycling genes universally present across all species, while nitrification and sulphite reduction genes were restricted to soil species. Halotolerant species shared Kdp potassium transporter and branched-chain amino acid pathways. Demonstrates niche-specific distribution of nutrient cycling genes in a bacterial genus. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1099/mgen.0.001108) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **Yin et al. (2022)** — Delftia tsuruhatensis pangenome analysis showing open pangenome, diverse mobile genetic elements, and phosphate transporter genes related to rhizosphere adaptation and plant growth promotion. HGT plays an important role in genetic diversity. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1128/spectrum.02072-21) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

- **Zhao et al. (2024)** — Comprehensive pangenome of 213 Stenotrophomonas strains (27,186 gene families). All strains harbored genes for phosphate solubilization and siderophore production. Phosphate-related and metal resistance genes may be acquired by HGT. Relevant as an example of co-occurring P and metal traits across a genus-level pangenome. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2024.1395477) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

### Theme 4: Iron-Phosphorus Geochemistry

- **Gallo et al. (2018)** — Klebsiella oxytoca DSM 29614 (isolated from acid mine drainage) has an open pangenome with small core genome (2% of total). Grows anaerobically on Fe(III)-citrate, reducing Fe(III) to Fe(II). Proteins for inorganic ion transport/metabolism (COG P) are particularly abundant. Demonstrates a direct genomic link between iron metabolism and phosphatase activity in metal-rich environments. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12866-018-1330-5) `[ABSTRACT ONLY]`

## Methods Comparison

| Study | Scale | Method | Key metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| This project | 27,682 species | Pangenome co-occurrence (Fisher, phi, permutation, Stouffer) | phi=0.110 (P×Metal) |
| Moulana et al. 2020 | 22 MAGs, 1 genus | Pangenome + selection analysis | dN/dS, gene frequency |
| Grote et al. 2012 | 6 genomes, 1 clade | Core genome conservation | 48-56% core |
| Tiruveedula & Wangikar 2017 | 23,643 genes, 1 phylum | Gene Conservation Index + PCA | GCI correlation |
| Xie et al. 2025 | 104 MAGs, 1 genus | Pangenome + metabolic modeling | Clade-specific gene gain/loss |
| Jung et al. 2023 | 7 species, 1 genus | Pangenome + HGT/CRISPR | 6,842 gene clusters |
| Zhao et al. 2024 | 213 strains, 1 genus | Pangenome + functional annotation | 27,186 gene families |

**Key methodological distinction**: Prior pangenome studies of nutrient gene variation operate within a single genus or phylum. The present project is the first to quantify macro-nutrient × trace-metal gene co-occurrence across the full GTDB species tree (27,682 species, multi-phylum), making it novel in both scale and cross-nutrient scope.

## Gaps in Current Knowledge

1. **No prior cross-kingdom pangenome co-occurrence study**: All existing nutrient-gene pangenome studies are genus- or phylum-level. The BERDL analysis is the first to test co-occurrence across >27,000 species spanning the bacterial tree.

2. **Phenazine-P coupling is mechanistically characterized but not genomically quantified**: McRose & Newman (2021) demonstrated the metabolite-level mechanism, but no study has asked whether phenazine operon genes and metal-handling genes show systematic genomic co-occurrence across diverse taxa.

3. **N-fixation × metal gene co-occurrence is unexplored**: While the nitrogenase FeMo-cofactor requirement is textbook knowledge, no genome-scale study has quantified co-occurrence of nif genes with metal transport genes across thousands of species.

4. **Environmental stratification of nutrient-metal coupling**: The project's finding that P×Metal is strongest in plant-associated/soil environments while N×Metal is strongest in marine/animal-associated environments is novel and not anticipated by prior literature.

5. **phzF × feoB anti-correlation**: The project's finding that phenazine producers avoid direct Fe(II) uptake (feoB) is a novel observation that extends the McRose-Newman model — phenazine-mediated Fe(III) reduction may make feoB redundant.
